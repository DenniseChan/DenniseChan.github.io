A Brief History of Time: From the Big Bang to Black Holes by Stephen Hawking

A book on theoretical cosmology by English physicist Stephen Hawking. It was first published in 1988. Hawking wrote the book for readers who had no prior knowledge of physics and people who are interested in learning something new about interesting subjects. Get it here.

Cosmos by Carl Sagan

A 1980 popular science book by astronomer and Pulitzer Prize-winning author Carl Sagan. Its 13 illustrated chapters, corresponding to the 13 episodes of the Cosmos TV series, which the book was co-developed with and intended to complement, explore the mutual development of science and civilization. Get it here.

NightWatch: A Practical Guide to Viewing the Universe by Terence Dickinson

A popular science book by Terence Dickinson. First published in 1983, there are more than 800,000 copies in print, making it the top-selling stargazing guide in the past 20 years. Each new edition has outsold the previous one. Get it here.
